F1 TECHNICAL & RACING GUIDE

⚡

Hybrid Power Units

Modern F1 cars use highly advanced hybrid power units, combining a turbocharged V6 internal combustion engine with powerful electric energy recovery systems (MGU-K and MGU-H) for maximum efficiency.

📐

Aerodynamics & DRS

Downforce is everything in F1. The wings and floor create massive grip through corners, while the Drag Reduction System (DRS) opens the rear wing on straights to reduce drag and increase top speed for overtaking.

🏁

Race Regulations & Flags

Drivers must obey strict track signaling. A Yellow flag warns of danger ahead, Red immediately stops the session for safety, and Blue orders a slower car to yield and let faster leaders pass.

OFFICIAL PIRELLI TYRE COMPOUNDS

SOFT (P-Zero Red)

The fastest tyre available. Provides maximum grip for qualifying and short stints, but degrades very quickly.

MEDIUM (P-Zero Yellow)

The most balanced option. Offers a great compromise between high performance and durability during the race.

HARD (P-Zero White)

The most durable compound. Perfect for long stints and hot track temperatures, but takes longer to warm up.
